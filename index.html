Single Family Homes across Cantons and Regions

Cut-off year: 2021

The following plot shows the number of Inhabitants and the fraction of Single Family Homes in respect to the total number of Buildings for each canton. When investigating this regional aspect, one can make several observations:

  • Cantons of Central Switzerland: All these cantons have lower proportion of Single Family Homes (all below 50 % of the total number of buildings).
  • Cantons of Eastern Switzerland and Lake Geneva Region: These cantons show the exact opposite to the cantons of Central Switzerland. The proportion of Single Family Homes is in general over 50 %
  • Big Cities: The two cantons of Zurich (ZH) and Bern (BE) are obvious outliers in this plot. Due to their big cities (e.g., Zurich, Winterthur, Bern, Thun) they lay far more on the right side of the plot and stand out compared to the other cantons.

These observations underline some facts that we already know: Firstly, the Cantons of Eastern Switzerland are known to be more rural and focused on agriculture and farming. Cities and villages are relatively small and less populated. This leads to the areas being less in demand and therefore, the price of land might be considerably more affordable when compared to other cantons (see more on that later -> Sabrina). Secondary, Cantons like Zug (ZG) have a lower tax load so hounding tends to be more expensive (see more on that later -> Sabrina).
