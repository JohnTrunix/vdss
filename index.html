Switzerland has fewer homeowners than most european countries

Homeownership Rate in 2018

The barchart shows the percentage of inhabitants that actually own their home in 2018. One might be suprised to find one of the financially strongest countries comes in last. Only 42.50% of the swiss are homeowners. Meaning most inhabitants rent their appartments. Until today this ratio has yet to change significantly.

One probable reasoning for the above mentioned positioning of Switzerland may be, that its average price for houses (CHF/m2) is higher than most countries. In Switzerland a buyer pays on average over 7000 CHF per square meter which is over 1.6 times the second most expensive country Norway.

real-estate prices across switzerland are higher than ever

the prices for buying real-estate are increasing at a higher rate than they are for renting

The already extremely high price of becoming a homeowner in switzerland is constantly increasing. The lineplot shows the development of the price per square meter since 2020. (01-01-2020 = 100%) Whilst the prices for rental appartments have 'only' increased by 5.2%, for condominiums and houses they have increased by approximatly 19% since 2020. So condominiums and houses getting more expensive faster than rental appartments.

Single Family Homes across Cantons

Cut-Off Year: 2021

  • Central Switzerland: All these cantons have lower proportion of Single Family Homes this means below 50 % of the total number of buildings.
  • Eastern Switzerland and Lake Geneva:The proportion of Single Family Homes is in general over 50 %
  • Big Cities: The cantons Zurich (ZH) and Bern (BE) are obvious outliers in this plot due to their big cities (e.g., Zurich, Winterthur, Bern, Thun) and many inhabitants. cantons.

These observations underline some facts:

  • Cantons of Eastern Switzerland are known to be more rural and focused on agriculture and farming. Cities and villages are relatively small and less populated. This leads to the areas being less in demand and therefore, the price of land might be considerably more affordable.
  • Cantons like Zug (ZG) have a lower tax load so hounding tends to be more expensive (see more on that later).

The following is a heatmap that shows which type of construction work costs how much in the regions of switzerland. A value of 0 % corresponds to the average cost in switzerland. Some categories like e.g. Windows exhibit very similar cost point within all regions but when it comes to sealings, flooring and carpentry work, one can observe big differences between the regions. An interesting point that can be observed, is that Easterns Switzerland has in general lower costs in may categories while building in the Lake Geneva Region is obviously more costy.
